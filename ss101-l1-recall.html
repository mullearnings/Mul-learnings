Part 1 · Getting started
What overall arc does SS 101 trace?
The Sikh tradition from Guru Nanak to Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth and final Guru — a tradition unfolding across history, studied in sequence.

What does "depth tracks effort" mean for this course?
Much of the intellectual and spiritual payoff is in the additional readings, not just the mandatory ones; reading is part of how depth forms, and the course also teaches whom to read.

Part 2 · History without colonial boxes
What does "precolonial religion was fluid, not boxed" mean?
Boundaries existed but were lived permeably — e.g. Muslim rababis openly performed the seva of kirtan in Darbar Sahib, visible into the 20th century.
Tap to reveal
According to Mandair, what is "religion" as we tend to understand it?
A largely 19th-century construct (shaped by forces like the colonial census) that locks people into sealed, mutually-exclusive categories.
Tap to reveal
Why must James Mill's three-age model be challenged?
It is imperial justification, not neutral history: Hindu golden age → Muslim "decline" → British "rescue." It falsely treats the medieval period as stagnant and chops history into sealed religious boxes.
Tap to reveal
What do the 15% / 10% figures show?
Muslims were ~15% of the subcontinent under the Mughals; Sikhs ~10% of Punjab under Ranjit Singh — minority rule, which complicates simple "who ruled whom" stories.
Tap to reveal
Name examples of Sikh–Mughal / Hindu entanglement (not just opposition).
The Sufi Mian Mir; the Janam Sakhi of Guru Nanak meeting Kabir; Guru Amar Das including Kabir and other Bhagats in the pothis feeding the Adi Granth.

Why not label "mixed"-looking precolonial material as "not real Sikhi"?
Because concepts, metaphors, and narratives are redefined and uniquely used by each community; "syncretic" is too blunt a word for it.

Part 3 · Guru Nanak in world history
What world events frame Guru Nanak's birth year, 1469?
The Gutenberg press, the Spanish Inquisition, Columbus crossing the Atlantic, and Luther's Reformation (all within his lifetime's span).
Tap to reveal
What political order ruled before the Mughals?
The Delhi Sultanate — Turko-Afghan regimes (the Lodhis among them) ruling mainly the north.
Tap to reveal
How did Babur win in 1526?
With new gunpowder weapons (topaks) and mounted technique against the Sultanate's war elephants (hasti) — an older style of warfare.
Tap to reveal
Fill the gaps: Babur → ___ → Akbar → Jahangir → ___ → Aurangzeb.
Humayun and Shah Jahan.
Tap to reveal
Which emperor ordered Guru Tegh Bahadur's execution — and who did Guru Gobind Singh actually meet?
Aurangzeb ordered the execution; Guru Gobind Singh met not Aurangzeb but his successor, Bahadur Shah.
Tap to reveal
Distinguish: rival claimant vs parallel tradition vs outside opponent.
Rival claimants = Prithi Chand & Ram Rai; a parallel tradition = the Udasis (Sri Chand); these are not the same as a simple outside opponent.

Part 4 · His life & the Janam Sakhis
Guru Nanak's dates and family?
1469–1539, born into the Bedī family, a Khatrī (merchant) caste household.
Tap to reveal
How should the Janam Sakhis be read?
As theological storytelling in the language of myth and allegory (Nikky-Guninder Kaur), with the shabad as the kernel — not as modern history textbooks.
Tap to reveal
What is Julie Vig's "hegemonic resistance"?
The narratives take a recognised sacred world and reorder it — placing all its prestigious figures (devas, siddhas, naths) beneath Guru Nanak. Not dilution, but theological reworking.
Tap to reveal
What does the Mecca story (feet toward the Kaaba) teach?
The Divine is omnipresent and formless (nirguna); Guru Nanak overturns an established sacred code to unsettle assumptions and reorient perception.
Tap to reveal
Vogel's three contrasts between Guru Nanak and the yogis?
Grace vs effort (sahaj); devotion vs technique (bhakti vs jugti); community vs solitude (sangat vs withdrawal).

Part 5 · The flavours of his writing
What is the central trinity of Guru Nanak's writing?
Nām, Dān, Ishnān — the Divine Name, charity, and cultivation of the body.
Tap to reveal
What is Naam — and why "experience over mastery"?
Not a narrow formula but loving devotion. Since the Divine is agam agochar (beyond mind and senses), the aim is experience through love, not conceptual capture.
Tap to reveal
Trace the ripening of devotion (the three kinds).
The nine practices → prema bhakti (love that grows sweeter, "red upon red") → non-duality (the self dissolves into the One).
Tap to reveal
How does Dān redefine purity?
Around honest labour and giving: the householder who earns and shares is "pure as the Ganges" (GGS 952). Later institutionalised as Daswandh.
Tap to reveal
What is Ishnān beyond literal bathing?
The cultivation of the body and the senses (rising at amrit vela; the five senses honed) — purity that is inner, not just ritual.
Tap to reveal
"Na koi Hindu, na Musalman" — what does it mean, and what follows from it?
Social identities are external; the deeper Divine unity is in all. From this follows social responsibility and langar's deliberate dismantling of caste.
Tap to reveal
Why is Gurbani composed in raag and meant to be sung?
Because it is meant to be experienced, not just read — the medium is part of the message; poetry and music are felt before they are paraphrased.
Tap to reveal
What is vismād?
Wondrous joy — the abiding mood of Guru Nanak's writing; the One shining through endless multiplicity, leaving him "perpetually inebriated" with wonder.
Tap to reveal
How does Guru Nanak use "fear" (bhau)?
Not as guilt before a punishing God, but as awe / the sublime (cf. Kant, Hume). Fear steadies love — "without fear, no one crosses over."
Tap to reveal
Part 6 · Nirguna & Sarguna
Nirguna vs Sarguna?
Nirguna = the Divine without form; Sarguna = the Divine with form.
Tap to reveal
If Gurbani uses "Ram" and "Hari," is it Vaishnava?
No — Guru Nanak writes the Divine as Nirguna (formless, unborn, eternal), which makes the writing accessible across communities. Sarguna imagery is repurposed, not bound to Vaishnava theology.
Tap to reveal
Guru Arjan's distinction: symbol vs identification?
Worship the symbol / virtue (the Divine quality the name expresses) — not the name as identification, which breeds sectarian rivalry. The first becomes nirvair, fearless.
