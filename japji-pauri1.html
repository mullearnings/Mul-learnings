Japji Sahib

Pauri 1 — Arth & Vyākhyā

✦ ✦ ✦
Prākathan — Pauri 1

The Four Failed Paths & The True Way

Preface — Bhai Vir Singh Having recited the Salok of "Aad Sach," Guru Ji now recites the first Pauri. In the Mool Mantar, attainment of Akaal Purakh was said to come through the Guru. Guru Ji now explains the means by which that attainment may actually be achieved. First, those methods are described which were tried and proved unsuccessful. Then, the successful method is revealed.

Line by Line

ਸੋਚੈ ਸੋਚਿ ਨ ਹੋਵਈ ਜੇ ਸੋਚੀ ਲਖ ਵਾਰ ॥
Arth (Bhai Vir Singh) & Interpretation
To attain the True Form, the wall of falsehood between us and Him must be broken. That wall cannot be broken by bodily rituals — because by performing ritual purification, purity of the mind is not attained, even if one were to cleanse oneself a hundred thousand times.
Washing the body cannot wash the mind. The barrier between you and Truth is inner — no external act can reach it.
ਚੁਪੈ ਚੁਪ ਨ ਹੋਵਈ ਜੇ ਲਾਇ ਰਹਾ ਲਿਵ ਤਾਰ ॥
Arth (Bhai Vir Singh) & Interpretation
One might think — if I simply stop speaking, falsehood is stopped and I have become truthful. To this it is explained: by remaining outwardly silent, the mind cannot be stilled from the restless pull of desire, even if one maintains an unbroken, steady inward focus.
Silence of the mouth is not silence of the mind. The ego does not stop chattering just because the lips do. Like the heron standing motionless at the water's edge — still outside, hungry and plotting inside.
ਭੁਖਿਆ ਭੁਖ ਨ ਉਤਰੀ ਜੇ ਬੰਨਾ ਪੁਰੀਆ ਭਾਰ ॥
Arth (Bhai Vir Singh) & Interpretation
One might think — if I observe fasts, I will become truthful. To this it is said: by fasting, the false hunger — the craving that dwells in desire — is never satiated, even if one were to receive the wealth of all fourteen realms as a result of those fasts.
Desire cannot be starved into silence. Craving lives in the ego, not the stomach. Give it more, and it wants more still. The nature of trishnaa is that it grows with feeding.
ਸਹਸ ਸਿਆਣਪਾ ਲਖ ਹੋਹਿ ਤ ਇਕ ਨ ਚਲੈ ਨਾਲਿ ॥
Arth (Bhai Vir Singh) & Interpretation
Even if one possesses thousands and hundreds of thousands of clever strategies — because they are all rooted in ego, they remain on this side alone. Not even one accompanies the soul to the realm of True Reality.
All intellectual achievement — scholarship, philosophy, debate, scripture — still runs on the engine of ego. The very "I am wise" that feels like progress is the wall. Cleverness can produce great speakers and writers, but it deepens haumai, not dissolves it.
ਕਿਵ ਸਚਿਆਰਾ ਹੋਈਐ ਕਿਵ ਕੂੜੈ ਤੁਟੈ ਪਾਲਿ ॥
Arth (Bhai Vir Singh) & Interpretation
Then tell us — how does one become truthful? And how is the wall of falsehood broken — the wall creating the distance between us and Him?
The turning point of the entire Pauri. Every door has been closed. Every method has failed. The question lands with full sincerity — not rhetorically, but as a genuine seeker who has exhausted the obvious paths.
ਹੁਕਮਿ ਰਜਾਈ ਚਲਣਾ ਨਾਨਕ ਲਿਖਿਆ ਨਾਲਿ ॥੧॥
Arth (Bhai Vir Singh) & Interpretation
O Nanak — by walking in accordance with the Hukam, the Will of the Divine which He has already inscribed within the very nature of the soul, one becomes truthful.
The answer is not another technique. It is surrender to what is already written within you. Hukam is not an external command to obey — it is the natural law woven into your being. The shift is from doing to aligning. From striving to recognizing.

The Four Failed Paths — Summary

Method Why It Fails Root Problem
Ritual purification
Washing, bathing, soch		 Cleans the body, not the mind External act
Silence / Moun
Stillness, meditation posture		 Quiets the mouth; inner craving keeps churning Ego still active
Fasting / Brat
Austerity, self-denial		 Starves the body; desire only grows Trishnaa deepens
Cleverness / Siyanap
Scholarship, philosophy, scripture		 Ego-driven; cannot reach beyond itself Haumai grows

The True Answer

ਹੁਕਮਿ ਰਜਾਈ ਚਲਣਾ

Walk in Hukam — the Will already written within you. Not a new technique. Not another effort. A recognition that the wall of falsehood (haumai) is what all self-directed effort feeds. True alignment comes not from doing more, but from surrendering the sense of a separate self that is doing the doing.

Vyākhyā — Commentary (Bhai Vir Singh)

Bhai Vir Singh identifies the single thread running through all four failed methods:

The Wall is Haumai (Ego)

The wall of falsehood (kooR dī pāl) standing between the seeker and the True Form is haumai — ego. It is an inner wall. No external act can reach it. In fact, ritualistic acts do not merely fail to remove haumai — they actively increase it.

ਕਰਮ ਕਰਤ ਬਦੇ ਅਹੰਮੇਵ ॥ "Through ritualistic actions, ego only grows." — Kabir Ji (Gauri)

On Silence (Moun)

Silence is a form of austerity (tap). But tap creates only outward stillness — inside, the mind remains agitated. Like the heron motionless at the water's edge, inwardly focused entirely on catching fish. The stream of thoughts driven by ego never stops simply because the mouth closes.

ਤਪੇ ਤਪੀਸਰ ਡੋਲੈ ਚੀਤ ॥ "Even the greatest ascetics — their minds still waver." — Kabir Ji (Gond)
ਬੋਲੈ ਨਾਹੀ ਹੋਇ ਬੈਠਾ ਮੋਨੀ ॥ ਅੰਤਰਿ ਕਲਪ ਭਵਾਈਐ ਜੋਨੀ ॥ "One sits silent, observing moun — yet inside, the mind keeps wandering through births." — M:5 (Prabhaati, Ashatpadi)

On Fasting (Brat)

Craving (trishnaa) arises from haumai. As long as haumai remains, trishnaa remains. Fasting does not uproot haumai — it only suppresses it temporarily. Moreover the reward promised for fasting in the scriptures is worldly things — but the nature of craving is: the more it receives, the more it wants.

ਭੁਖਿਆ ਭੁਖ ਨ ਉਤਰੈ ਗਲੀ ਭੁਖ ਨ ਜਾਇ ॥ ਨਾਨਕ ਭੁਖਾ ਤਾ ਰਜੈ ਜਾ ਗੁਣ ਕਹਿ ਗੁਣੀ ਸਮਾਇ ॥ "The hungry are not satisfied by talking. O Nanak, the hungry one is satisfied only when they sing the virtues of the Virtuous One and merge in Him." — Var Majh, M:2. The only true satiation is merger in the Source.

On Cleverness (Siyanap)

Siyanap — the wonders of the intellect — whether personal sharpness, study of scriptures, or wisdom from learned company — makes one a speaker, a writer, a debater. But it does not dissolve haumai. It inflates it. The True Form is beyond the reach of mind and intellect entirely.

ਹਮ ਬਡ ਕਬਿ ਕੁਲੀਨ ਹਮ ਪੰਡਿਤ ਹਮ ਜੋਗੀ ਸੰਨਿਆਸੀ ॥ ਗਿਆਨੀ ਗੁਨੀ ਸੂਰ ਹਮ ਦਾਤੇ ਇਹ ਬੁਧਿ ਕਬਹਿ ਨ ਨਾਸੀ ॥ "I am a great poet, of noble lineage, a scholar, a yogi, a renunciant. I am wise, virtuous, brave, charitable — this ego-intellect never perishes." — Ravidas Ji. The very list of achievements is the trap.
ਸਹਸ ਸਿਆਣਪ ਨਹ ਮਿਲੈ ਮੇਰੀ ਜਿੰਦੁੜੀਏ ॥ ਜਨ ਨਾਨਕ ਗੁਰਮੁਖਿ ਜਾਤਾ ਰਾਮ ॥ "Through thousands of clever strategies, O my soul, the Lord is not found. Servant Nanak knows Him only as Gurmukh." — M:4, Bihagra

✦ Reflection Pointers
  • When thoughts, desires, and emotions rise and fall — how often do you pause to observe their movement rather than letting them define who you believe yourself to be?
  • If silence, discipline, accumulation, and knowledge do not settle the mind — what does this reveal about the limitations of effort-based approaches to inner clarity?
  • Every method that failed was driven by you doing it. What would it mean to stop being the one driving — and simply walk in Hukam?
