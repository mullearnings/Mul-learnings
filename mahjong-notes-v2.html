Personal Study Notes · Singaporean Variant

Mah Jong

136 tiles · 4 players
3 suits · Winds · Dragons
✦ = confirm with teacher
Objective 4 melds + 1 pair = 14 tiles. A meld is a group of 3 (or 4) tiles. The pair is 2 identical tiles — the "eyes." Win by completing your hand first. Kong adds 1 tile — draw replacement from back of wall
Meld Types
Chow Chi · Run · Sequence
3 consecutive tiles, same suit. Numbered suits only — not winds or dragons.
Claim only from player to your left.
Pung Triplet
3 identical tiles. Any tile type — suits, winds, and dragons all valid.
Claim from any player. Call "Pung!"
Kong Quad
4 identical tiles. Counts as one meld. Draw replacement from back of wall.
Claim from any player. Call "Kong!"
Pair Eyes
2 identical tiles. Exactly one pair required to complete any hand.
Always concealed. Claim any discard to win.
Discard Strategy
Keep close tiles Tiles like 4-5 or 3-5 in the same suit are nearly a sequence — keep them.
Discard isolated tiles first A lone 1 or 9 with no neighbors is hard to use — discard it early.
Watch the discard pile If three 7-Dots are already discarded, the 4th is safe — nobody can pung or kong it.
Read open melds If a player shows a pung of Red Dragons, they're collecting honors — avoid discarding dragons near them.
