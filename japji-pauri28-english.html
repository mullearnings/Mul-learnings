The Bridge — Closing Lines of Pauri 27

Annie Auntie opened this lesson by pointing to the two lines that close Pauri 27. They are not merely a poetic ending — they are the hinge between 27 pauris of theory and the practice that follows.

ਸੋਈ ਸੋਈ ਸਦਾ ਸਚੁ ਸਾਹਿਬੁ ਸਾਚਾ ਸਾਚੀ ਨਾਈ ॥
ਹੈ ਭੀ ਹੋਸੀ ਜਾਇ ਨ ਜਾਸੀ ਰਚਨਾ ਜਿਨਿ ਰਚਾਈ ॥

He and he alone is ever the same, ever the truth. The master is true and true is his glory. He is, he ever shall be, he shall never depart — he who created this creation.

Everything in creation changes. Only Akal Purakh remains constant. It also connects directly to ਅਜੂਨੀ ਸੈਭੰ from the Mool Mantar — neither born nor dying, self-existent. This is the ground from which Pauris 28–31 ask: so how do we actually live?

The Full Context
This tab covers the complete context behind Pauri 28 — who Guru Nanak is speaking to, why this conversation happened, the Siddha Ghosh, and the philosophical ground of Grihastha life. It also maps the transition from 27 pauris of theory into daily practice.
Siddha Ghosh

Who Is Guru Nanak Talking To?

Pauris 28–31 come from a real historical conversation. Guru Nanak is speaking directly to the yogis and Siddhas — the recognized wise men of that era. These were not ordinary people. Society looked to them for counsel and considered them spiritually advanced. They had a role, an authority, and a very specific outward appearance.

The full record of this exchange is preserved in the Guru Granth Sahib as Siddha Ghosh — Guru Nanak's formal dialogue with the Siddhas. One important detail Annie Auntie emphasized: nobody recorded it in real time. It was written from Guru Nanak's memory after the conversation was complete.

The yogis wore earrings, carried begging bowls, smeared ashes on their bodies, wore patched coats, carried walking sticks. They renounced the world, went to forests and mountains, and expected to be fed by society. Guru Nanak's aim was not to condemn them. His aim was to ask — what are these symbols actually pointing to? And then to say: stop wearing the pointer. Start embodying what it points to.

One Truth for All

Why This Teaching Is Universal

Annie Auntie

"He is telling them that Akal Purakh is the only abiding truth. And since religion has to deal with this one abiding truth, it must basically be the same for all humanity. Because all humanity has one creation. One Akal Purakh that has created the whole creation. So every religion comes under it."

If there is one creator, and all human beings are created by the same one, then their spiritual needs are fundamentally the same — regardless of race, religion, or nationality. The differences between religions are real. But the human being inside every religion is the same.

This is why Guru Nanak could speak to yogis, to Hindus, to Muslims — without asking anyone to convert. He was not teaching a religion. He was pointing at the human being underneath the religion.

The Core Distinction

Religion Divides. Spirituality Unites.

Key Distinction

Religion vs. Spirituality

Religion is a set of external practices, dogmas, calendars, and symbols. It organises people — and in doing so, it also divides them. You eat this on Tuesday but not that. You go to this building. You belong to this group. Religion places power in the hands of those who make the rules.

Spirituality is the inner work of becoming — more aware, more free from ego, more connected to the one underlying reality. It requires no institution, no symbol, no calendar. Only honest inward effort.

Guru Nanak said: everybody is equal. He did not position himself as the teacher everyone must follow. He farmed his land after his Udaasis. He said — I am living like you. You live like me.

And yet — the physical place is not the problem. Going to a mandir, church, or gurdwara is not wrong. The question is always: are you becoming a better version of yourself while you are there?

Student (Shirdi)
"Does this principle mean you should not be going to church and mandirs?"
Annie Auntie
"It does not matter where you are. What matters is — wherever you are, are you learning something to become a better version of yourself? I have been to churches, I have been to mandirs, I have attended masses and pujas. The place does not matter. My thinking matters. I could be in hell, I could be in jail, I could still become a better version of myself. I could be living in a palace and still be the lowest version of myself. What was happening on Epstein Island? It was the richest place. It was a palace. But where were those people living?"

The Grihastha Life

You Cannot Run from Yourself

The yogis believed the world was a source of sin and suffering, and the right response was to leave it — go to the mountains, meditate away from society. Guru Nanak disagreed fundamentally.

The Gutter in the Man

"You can get away from the world, but the world doesn't get away from you. You can get the man out of the gutter, but can you get the gutter out of the man? You could take me from here to a beautiful palace in the hills. I am taking my same mind, my same thoughts, my same struggles over there."

The mountain does not heal the mind. The forest does not dissolve the ego. You bring yourself with you wherever you go. This is why Guru Nanak taught the Grihastha life — live in the world, earn your living, share people's joys and sorrows as one of them.

Guru Nanak referenced this directly from Rag Maru:

ਕਰਕਰਿ ਮੰਗਣਿ ਜਗ ਪਰਿ ਭਉਤਲਿ ਮਨਿ ਅੰਧੇ ਅਭਿਧਾਰੀ ॥ They beg from door to door across the world, yet instruct others as if they are the wisest — blind in the mind, unable to see that they themselves need help. Rag Maru, Guru Nanak Dev Ji

And the one road that leads to the one door — from Rag Ram Malhar:

ਇਕੋ ਪੰਧੁ ਇਕੁ ਦਰੁ ਗੁਰ ਪਉੜੀ ਨਿਜਠਾਣੁ ॥ ਸਭ ਸੁਖ ਸਚੁ ਨਾਮ ॥ There is only one road, one door. The Guru is the ladder to that true home. All bliss is in the true Name. Rag Ram Malhar, Guru Nanak Dev Ji

The Transition

27 Pauris of Theory. Now: Practice.

Annie Auntie

"Till now, Guru Nanak has talked about Omkar and acceptance through the past 27 bodies. He talked about Amrit Vela. He talked about Tirath — the internal pilgrimage, cleansing from within. He talked about Gaavai — sing the praise and gratitude. He talked about Mandai — accept the Hukam, be in acceptance, let life unfold. Now we have a lot of theory. But from here, he talks about putting it into practice. Implementation starts and the way of day-to-day living. It keeps getting deeper and deeper from here."

The Central Move

Inside = Outside. No Duality.

Guru Nanak was deeply and consistently opposed to duality — the gap between what is shown on the outside and what is actually happening on the inside. This runs through all of his teachings without exception.

Annie Auntie

"He wanted us to be — whatever is inside is outside. No double-facedness. He wanted the outside and the inside to be one. He has always criticized duality. And he has been in support of oneness. All his teachings lead us to oneness. The very word Ekamkar emphasizes that. As long as we live in duality — as long as we have I and my ego — I cannot become Ekamkar. I have to shed the duality to be one with the oneness."

The yogis were the living embodiment of this problem. Their outer appearance was supposed to communicate an inner state of renunciation and humility. But the appearance had become a substitute for the state. They wore the symbol without living the reality it pointed to. Guru Nanak does not destroy the symbols. He internalizes them.

The Full Substitution

Symbol by Symbol

Symbol Gurmukhi Replace With The Deeper Meaning
Earrings ਮੁੰਦਾ Contentment (Santokh) The earring is always worn. Make contentment your permanent ornament — at every moment, not just for occasions.
Begging Bowl ਝੋਲੀ Hard Work — Earn Your Own Living Our effort is the real bowl. We hold it out and the creator fills it with grace. But we must do the work first.
Ashes ਬਿਭੂਤਿ Awareness (Dhiaan) Instead of physical ashes, smear yourself in gratitude and the constant inner presence of the Parmatma.
Patched Coat ਖਿੰਥਾ Cloak of Death (Kaal) Wearing awareness of death means: I am ready, no unfinished business, already living fully. Not grief — liberation from attachment.
Walking Stick ਡੰਡਾ Trust and Faith (Parteet) The stick prevents falling. Faith and trust in the creator is the only support that truly holds you upright.
Yogic Technique ਜੁਗਤਿ Purity — Body and Mind Physical purity through diet and self-restraint. Mental purity through detachment from past and future. The mind is harder to purify than the body.

The Philosophical Reversal

The Soul Was Given a Body — Not the Other Way Around

Annie Auntie

"The soul has come on this earth to complete a journey. The soul is given a body to complete the journey. It is not that this body has been given a soul to complete the journey. The soul has been given a body to complete the journey."

Most of us live as if we are a body that happens to have a soul. This makes the body primary — so we spend 80–90% of our life serving it. Guru Nanak reverses this entirely. You are a soul. The body is a temporary vehicle given to complete what you came here to complete. Don't mistake the vehicle for the passenger.

ਮਰਨਾ ਲਿਖਿਆ ਮੰਡਲ ਮਹਿ ਆਏ ॥ Death was written before we came into this world. Gurbani — Marnaa likhiaa, then you came. Not the other way. Death is not an interruption. It is the design.

The Dalai Lama

"We live like we will never die, and then we die like we have never lived."

Annie Auntie brought this in directly. Awareness of death is not morbid — it is the most clarifying thing there is. It removes every false priority instantly. To wear the cloak of death is not grief. It is readiness.

The Sharpest Formulation

Mr. Doing vs. Mr. Being

Annie Auntie

"The external is only identity. It relates to ego. You, the being, is more important than you, the doing. 'I am an engineer. I do this. I dress this way. I drive this car. I live in this neighbourhood.' It is Mr. Doing. Can we become Mr. Being — and just be ourselves while we are doing everything?"

We have constructed entire identities out of our roles, achievements, and possessions. These are all doing. They are the outer garments — the earrings and ashes of modern life. Mr. Being is underneath all of that. It is the soul — the awareness that exists before the career, before the title, before the car.

The word yogi itself means union of the soul with the Parmatma. That union cannot happen through outer symbols. It happens at the level of Being, not Doing. The practice: Can I be fully present and aware while also doing everything that needs to be done? Not instead of the doing — alongside it.

Key Concepts Defined

In Annie Auntie's Own Words

Key Concept

Guru — ਗੁਰੂ

ਗੁ = darkness. ਰੁ = light. Anything — or anyone — that takes you from darkness to light, from ignorance to awareness. Not only a person. It could be two lines you read in the morning, a walk, a stranger's words that create sudden clarity. "If I ask you to pick up something from a dark room, you will ask me to switch on the light first. You need that awareness-light to see the truth. Without it, you live only in your perceptions."

Key Concept

Dharam — ਧਰਮ

Not religion. Not ritual. A way of life. To do the right thing, at the right time, for the right reasons, with the right intentions. No expectation attached. Pure Seva. The moment expectation enters, it may be deceit dressed as righteousness. And timing matters — sometimes the moment is lost. You were thinking about doing the right thing and someone else already did it. Dharam requires presence as much as intention.

Key Concept

Tirath — ਤੀਰਥ

Not a physical pilgrimage. Going inside ourselves and cleansing what has accumulated there — hatred, perceptions, judgment, doubt. Not going to Harmandir Sahib and bathing and coming back. What have you cleaned doing that? The real Tirath is internal. And the critical point: we can only do this with His grace. However much effort you make, the mind will say "I'm fine, I'm already good." The grace has to come first to allow the seeing.

Key Concept

Suniye & Maniye — ਸੁਣੀਐ ਤੇ ਮੰਨੀਐ

Suniye — to truly listen. Not just hear. We learn only through listening, reading, and observing. We cannot will ourselves into purity overnight.

Maniye — to truly accept and act. To let what was heard reshape how you live. Suniye opens the door. Maniye walks through it.

When reciting Paath, the Suniye gets deeper layer by layer. The same line means something different at 25, at 45, at 65. This is why daily practice is non-negotiable.

The Teaching Tools

Annie Auntie's Analogies — Preserved in Full

Analogy 1 — The Gutter in the Man

"You can get the man out of the gutter, but can you get the gutter out of the man? You could take me from here to a beautiful palace in the hills and say — enjoy, be one with the Paramatma. I am taking my same mind, my same thoughts, my same struggles over there."

The mountain does not fix you. The same mind travels with you wherever you go. The work is always internal — and it requires the friction of the world to do it properly. This is why the Grihastha life, not renunciation, is Guru Nanak's path.

Analogy 2 — The Child at Breakfast

"The child does not sit at the breakfast table and say — 'Mama, I'm going to be hungry later, let me put this toast in my pocket.' It says — I'm done, I'm full. Because it has faith that when the time comes, it will eat. It has complete trust."

The child is Nirbhau Nirvair — without fear, without enmity. It does not judge based on the past. It has no anxiety about the future. It trusts completely. This is the state Guru Nanak describes when he speaks of faith as the walking stick.

Analogy 3 — Chindi and the Sunday Drive

"I called Chindi and said — 'Chindi, I'm coming to London this Sunday and I need to go to a few places. Can you pick me up?' Chindi's faith says — I'll take you wherever you want to go. Or Chindi can say yes on the phone, put it down, and think — I don't know where she's taking me. I don't know if I want to go."

Doubt creates hesitation. Trust creates faith. Doubt is not entirely bad — it helps us find the right path. But too much doubt blinds us to the path itself. Always watch: am I in doubt or in faith right now?

Analogy 4 — The One Bedroom House (Pancha Vasai)

"You have one bedroom. I am visiting you — and then Godly phones. She says, 'Chindi, I am coming.' And you tell me — 'Annie, you've been here two months. Now Godly is coming. Can you make room? She brings peace. She brings love. She brings order.' You move the chaos out by moving me out."

This is Pancha Vasai. The five vices — ਕਾਮ, ਕ੍ਰੋਧ, ਲੋਭ, ਮੋਹ, ਅਹੰਕਾਰ — take up room. When the five virtues begin to settle inside, there is simply no space left for the vices. They leave on their own. You don't suppress them. You just make room for what is better. Then ਨਗਰੁ ਬਸਾ ਗੁਰ ਬੁੱਧਿ ਜੀਓ — a beautiful world is created.

Analogy 5 — The Langar Server

"We go to the Gurdwara and somebody important is serving langar. Half of us say — 'He's just trying to make an impression, he does all bad things.' Some of us say — 'Wow, he's doing Seva. Maybe he's turning his life around.'"

It's our perception. What comes to our mind reveals where we are living — not where he is. The person serving langar is not the subject here. Your response is.

The Closing Teaching

Pancha Vasai — The Five Virtues Moving In

ਪੰਚ ਵਸੇ ਪੰਚ ਪਰਧਾਨ ॥ ਨਗਰੁ ਬਸਾ ਗੁਰ ਬੁੱਧਿ ਜੀਓ ॥ The five virtues dwell and reign. A beautiful world is created through the Guru's wisdom. Guru Arjan Dev Ji

Daily Practice — Given by Annie Auntie

  1. Take a notebook. Open it fresh each day.
  2. Write down words for the five qualities you want to embody. Start simply — Good. Better. Best. Let the superlative degree keep changing as your awareness shifts.
  3. On good days you may write something high. On low days you may step back. That is honest. That is real practice.
  4. Do not add out of ego. Sit with what comes. Close it. Take it into the day.
  5. Over time, watch what words appear. They will shift and deepen. They will reveal where your awareness actually is — not where you think it is.
  6. The goal: ਪੰਚ ਪਰਵਾਣ ਪੰਚ ਪਰਧਾਨ ਪੰਚੇ ਪਾਵਹਿ ਦਰਗਹਿ ਮਾਨੁ — the five are accepted, the five are honoured, the five receive grace in the Divine Court.
Reflection Questions
  • A friend says: "I go to the Gurdwara every single day, I must be becoming more spiritual." Based on this pauri — what would Guru Nanak say back, and why?
  • Annie Auntie says the child is Nirbhau Nirvair. What specifically does the child not do that makes it that way? Can you identify a moment today when you did the opposite?
  • Which symbol in the substitution table points to an inner quality you currently feel most deficient in? What would it actually look like to wear that quality today?
  • Think of a time you changed your environment expecting to feel different — and found you had brought yourself along. What does that reveal?
  • If the soul was given a body to complete a journey — not the other way around — what would change about how you spend tomorrow?
  • What is the difference between Mr. Doing and Mr. Being in your life right now? Name three things you are overidentified with. What remains when you remove those labels?
